Viditor

Viditor, a portmanteau of video editor, is a fully featured opensource video development environment, but implemented entirely as a webservice. With the internet becoming increasingly ubiquitous, the applications we previously installed locally on our computers will be accessed remotely on the internet, including anything and everything from text processors to media players, or even video editors!

Viditor will be capable of rendering together videos in variety of formats, from MP4 to MOV to AVI, and will be accessible from a variety of platforms, from computers to tablets to phones. Viditor will also provide all users with synchronous access to their projects, where they can collaborate with others as they develop their projects, and manage their assets from a secondary server through a cloudesque service.

A prototype of the project is intended to be submitted by 3/22/2013 to the GigTank. Only by establishing a gigabyte connection could our servers handle all the high definition large aspect ratio video files that are processed across the internet, which would otherwise be impossibly slow to upload and download!

The project is developed entirely through the web languages that are natively supported by internet browsers, with HTML and CSS under JS. And on the server, which is currently nothing more than my new RPi, we use PHP and SQL along with ffMPEG. We are also very interested in experimenting with other techonologies, such as NodeJS or Dart!

Viditor is going to be absolutely awesome; but not without your contributions! If you want to join the development of the project, visit us on github and fork off our code! If you have any questions or comments, you can contact us at viditor@geoncode.com. Thanks!
